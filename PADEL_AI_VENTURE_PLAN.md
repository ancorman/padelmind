## 1. Mission & Vision

### Mission
**Make world-class padel coaching universally accessible — by turning every smartphone-equipped player into a data-driven student of the game, and every club into a smart venue.**

### Vision (10-year)
The default AI coaching infrastructure for padel in Asia. The system every serious player checks after a match the way runners check Strava and chess players check Lichess. A category-defining brand born in India that exports to the world — not the other way round.

### What we will NOT be
- A recording-and-storage service (commoditized, no moat, no defensibility)
- A booking app (Hudle owns that lane in India; we partner, we don't compete)
- A scoreboard / scoring app (saturated, free alternatives)
- A B2C consumer-only app without club distribution (impossible CAC at our scale)

### 4.5 Reality check — what are the real moats? (added 2026-07-01)

Pre-China analysis was too optimistic about tech as a moat. Hard truth:

**MOATS THAT DO NOT WORK against capable Chinese/global competition:**
- ❌ AI quality (commodity ML pieces glued together; CV talent is global)
- ❌ Hardware (Chinese ODMs make the cameras anyway)
- ❌ Pricing (Chinese players can match or beat us at any tier)
- ❌ Dataset size (volume can be matched within 12 months by anyone funded)

**MOATS THAT DO WORK:**

| Moat | Strength | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **India geopolitical wall** | **HIGH** | India app bans (TikTok 2020, PUBG, WeChat, SHEIN-class) extend to Chinese sports SaaS. Indian clubs face procurement risk choosing Chinese tech. **This is our shield in India.** |
| **Federation distribution lock** | **HIGH (if won early)** | Asian Games 2026 catalysed national padel federations forming NOW (India, UAE, KSA, SE Asia). Lock procurement at federation level → blocks Chinese entry by policy. |
| **Coach co-founder + domain rubric** | **MEDIUM** | A WPT-trained-coach-led rubric is hard to replicate without their own sport credibility hire. Buys 12–18 months. |
| **WhatsApp-native distribution + Hindi/Arabic UX depth** | **MEDIUM** | AX-style cultural-distribution muscle Chinese SaaS rarely matches. |

**Strategic question this raises (logged in Open Questions):** Should we deliberately be "India + Middle East only", explicitly anti-China by geography, and partner-or-license China rather than compete? Defending one geographic moat well > losing across three.

## 5. Product Strategy

### 5.1 Three product surfaces — sequenced

**Surface 1: Club System (Year 1 focus, B2B2C)**
- IP camera + edge buffer + cloud AI per court
- Club dashboard: court usage, recording index, sponsor overlay tools, player engagement
- Player PWA: post-match feedback, shot histogram, skill score, drill recommendations
- **Pricing:** ₹3,000–7,000/court/mo subscription; hardware ₹15–20k upfront or rolled into 24-mo plan

**Surface 2: Coach Tool (Year 2, B2C2B)**
- Coach-facing dashboard: review labelled clips from their students; assign drills; track progress over months
- **The lock-in.** Once a coach has 30 students on the platform, their workflow is on us
- **Pricing:** ₹500–1,500/coach/mo, or free if they bring N students (acquisition lever)

**Surface 3: Player Skill Identity (Year 2–3, B2C)**
- The "Clutch Score" equivalent that follows a player across clubs and cities
- Cross-club leaderboards; skill-matched matchmaking; tournament seeding feed
- **The network effect.** Hudle has bookings; we have *skill identity* — these are complementary, not competing
- **Pricing:** Freemium. Free tier = score + 5 analyses/mo. Pro = ₹399/mo, unlimited

## 6. Technical Architecture & Feasibility

### 6.1 The stack (validated by `padel_analytics` audit done 2026-07-01)

```
[IP Camera @ court]
    │ RTSP H.265
    ▼
[Edge Buffer — Raspberry Pi 5 + 1TB NVMe]
    │ Match-end trigger uploads MP4
    ▼
[Cloudflare R2 — match storage]
    │
    ▼
[RunPod / Lambda GPU worker]
  - YOLOv8m player detection
  - YOLOv8-pose keypoints
  - TrackNet ball tracking
  - Court homography (from 12 fixed keypoints clicked at install)
  - Shot classifier (our trained model) ← THE MOAT
  - Skill scoring engine ← THE MOAT
    │
    ▼
[Supabase: structured analytics + player identity]
    │
    ▼
[Player PWA — Cloudflare Pages] + [Club dashboard]
    │
    ▼
[WhatsApp delivery — AX router or Twilio]
```

### 6.2 Feasibility verdict (per repo audit)

| Layer | Risk | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Camera + RTSP | Low | Off-the-shelf Reolink RLC-823A handles 4K RTSP. Validated by hobbyist sports projects |
| Court homography | Low | `padel_analytics` already supports click-once-cache-forever flow |
| Player tracking | Low | YOLOv8m + ByteTrack is a solved problem |
| Pose keypoints | Low | YOLOv8-pose is mature |
| Ball tracking | Medium | TrackNet works but needs retraining on padel-specific data (ball lighter colour vs tennis) |
| **Shot classification** | **HIGH** | **No OSS solution exists. Must train. ~70% V1 target.** This is the project's central technical risk |
| Skill scoring | Medium | Composite of pose-quality + outcome + context. Algorithmic, not ML, but tricky to tune to coach intuition |
| Streaming refactor of `padel_analytics` | Medium | Batch-style today; ~3 weeks of work to make single-pass-per-frame |
| Mobile PWA | Low | Standard React work |

### 6.3 Hardware bill of materials — per court

| Item | Spec | INR |
|---|---|---|
| Camera | Reolink RLC-823A 16x (4K, PoE, IP66) — or Hikvision premium variant for 50% more | 12,000–22,000 |

| Edge buffer | Raspberry Pi 5 8GB + 1TB NVMe + case | 15,000 |

## 10. Go-to-Market Plan

### 10.1 Year 1 — 3 cities, 25 clubs, 5,000 players

**Beachhead: Mumbai → Pune → Bangalore.** Why: highest court density, highest disposable income, strongest WhatsApp + tech-adoption signals.

**Acquisition wedge — "Free pilot for top 3 clubs per city":**
- We install hardware + run analytics free for 60 days
- Club brings 30+ regular players who try the system
- After 60 days: club pays subscription OR we remove hardware (low-stakes for them)
- Players who tasted the feedback don't want to lose it → club gets pressured by demand
- This is the same playbook Hudle used to seed its booking dominance

**Distribution levers:**
1. **Hudle partnership** — explicit ask: in-app push to players in clubs we've covered, in exchange for revenue share or data
2. **WhatsApp-native funnel** — player gets feedback link via WhatsApp post-match; one tap to view, share, save → classic AX-style funnel
3. **Coach affiliate program** — coaches earn 10% recurring on every player they refer; this creates a sales force we don't pay salary for
4. **Tournament sponsorships** — sponsor 1–2 local circuits, get sole AI camera + livestream rights, build brand

### 10.2 Year 2 — UAE entry

**Why UAE first (not KSA):**
- English-default operating language
- High-density (950+ courts in a small geography)
- Expat-heavy player base = high willingness to pay
- Easier business setup than KSA
- Bridge to KSA via DIFC/ADGM-based operations

**Entry approach:** Acquire or partner with 1 existing UAE court operator chain (think 20-court operator) as launch customer. We become their tech stack, they become our distribution.

### 10.3 Year 3 — KSA + SE Asia

- KSA via Vision-2030 sport-infra RFPs; bid through UAE-registered subsidiary
- SE Asia (Indonesia, Thailand) via partnership with local franchise operators
- EU/LATAM pilots only if explicitly requested by investors (no own-cash spend)

### 12.5 Failure modes that could kill the venture (existential)

1. **Coach quality of judgment doesn't translate to algorithm.** The classifier hits 85% accuracy but real players say "this is wrong, I'm not playing badly" — losing trust kills WoM. *Mitigation: invest heavily in human-in-the-loop feedback before scaling.*
2. **Hudle builds it or partners with Clutch directly.** Distribution moat collapses. *Mitigation: get to Hudle BEFORE Clutch does. They are in Mumbai.*
3. **Founder burnout — Manoj cannot sustain AX + this.** *Mitigation: be honest about bandwidth; raise pre-seed quickly so a founding engineer can take ops load.*
4. **Hardware reliability nightmare.** Cameras failing in monsoon, dust, summer heat → support ticket avalanche. *Mitigation: spend Y1 obsessing over hardware QA; standardize one camera model + thermal-tested install kits.*
5. **Players don't actually share/talk about the feedback.** No viral loop → CAC stays high → unit economics break. *Mitigation: design feedback to be screenshot-worthy and WhatsApp-shareable from day one. Vanity-friendly visuals.*
