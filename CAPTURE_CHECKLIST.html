Court Capture Checklist

Two recordings, two setups. Both use the iPhone rear camera (never the selfie/front one — it's lower quality).

Keep this open on your phone court-side.

📷 Match capture Wide · locked

Feeds heatmaps, distance, rallies, highlights. Needs the whole court, camera never moving.
  • Rear camera, 1080p304K30 fine if storage allows — it downsamples anyway
  • Elevated, behind one baselinehigh on a tripod/clamp — not handheld
  • WHOLE court in frame — including the NEAR baselinethe #1 miss last time. Back up / raise / go wider until both back walls + all 4 corners are visible
  • Lock focus + exposuretap-and-hold the screen until "AE/AF LOCK" shows
  • Camera dead-still — do NOT touch itfrom calibration to the last point. If it moves, calibration breaks
  • Record 30 sec of the EMPTY court firstthat still = your one-time calibration frame
  • One continuous takeno stopping, no re-framing, no montage
  • Well-lit courtdim = detection fails. Lights on, no dark half

🎾 Pose capture Close · front-on

Feeds the coach's technique labeling. Needs ONE player, close and clear.
  • Rear camera, 1080p30
  • Court-level, FACING the player from the front/sidenot behind them — you want to see the swing and the body
  • Close — player fills ~half the frame heightthe closer and clearer, the better the pose
  • Nothing between camera and playerno net, no glass in the way if you can help it
  • Lock focus + exposure · steady mount
  • 2–3 minutes of hitting10–15 shots is plenty for a labeling batch

⛔ The 5 things that ruin a capture

  • ✕
    Moving the camera mid-recordingbreaks the whole calibration + tracking
  • ✕
    Cutting off the near baselinethen the near corners can't be calibrated
  • ✕
    Shooting through mesh / glassobscures players — detection drops
  • ✕
    A dim or half-lit courtthe detector needs to see the players
  • ✕
    Letting autofocus huntalways AE/AF lock before recording

If you remember one line

Rear camera. Whole court, near baseline included. Lock focus. Never move it. Lights on. Thirty seconds of empty court at the start is your calibration — then let it run.
