## Why this email works

1. **Opens with peer credibility** — AutomationXpert credentials in the first line establish I'm not a student / fanboy / vendor pitching to him.
2. **Shows I read his code** — specific mention of the four-tracker architecture, 13-DOF pose, TrackNet, 12-keypoint homography. This is the difference between a generic outreach and a "this person took my work seriously" email.
3. **Names the gap honestly** — calling out the missing classifier is something he already knows; saying it out loud builds trust that I understand the technical reality.
4. **Names the moat honestly** — the classifier is the commercial moat of the entire category. Acknowledging that openly says "I value what you'd contribute, this isn't a free-OSS-extraction play."
5. **Three concrete collaboration shapes** — gives him agency. The third option ("something else you'd propose") is the most important; it signals I'm not arriving with a rigid term sheet.
6. **The base club + 200 players signal** — concrete demand evidence, not market hand-waving.
7. **Time-zone flexibility** — removes any "is this person going to ask me to wake up at 5am?" friction.
8. **Graceful exit clause** — "if it's not the right fit" — reduces the social cost of replying no. Higher reply rate.
9. **The WhatsApp P.S.** — distinctive, memorable. Most cold founder emails read identically; this one has a personality fingerprint.
