## 1. Executive Summary

We are building **India's first AI-powered coaching platform for racket sports** — starting with padel, expanding into tennis, badminton, and pickleball. The platform records every match at partner clubs via a single fixed IP camera per court, analyses player technique with computer vision, scores each shot against an expert coach's rubric, and delivers personalised improvement feedback to the player on WhatsApp within ten minutes of match end.

**The thesis:** Racket-sports analytics — what Hawk-Eye is to professional tennis — is becoming a club-and-recreational-player product. Padel is the wedge because it is the fastest-growing sport in the world (1,026% search interest growth since 2004), has zero India-headquartered competition, and enters the 2026 Asian Games — a once-per-sport-lifecycle institutional catalyst.

**The business model:** B2B2C SaaS. Clubs pay per-court subscription (₹5,000/month) for analytics and player engagement; players access a free tier and can upgrade to Pro (₹399/month) for unlimited analyses and cross-club skill identity.

**The path:** 25 clubs in Mumbai/Pune/Bangalore in Year 1, expanding to 200 clubs across India and a UAE foothold in Year 2, then KSA and SE Asia in Year 3. **Anti-China by geography** — defensible through India's procurement-policy moat against Chinese SaaS.

**The team:** Manoj Maheshwari (CEO; AutomationXpert SMB-SaaS founder, established WhatsApp-distribution operator) + Padel Coach (CCO; domain authority) + CTO/ML Co-founder (in process) + Apple AI advisor (in process).

**The ask:** ₹2.5 Cr ($300k) pre-seed for 18 months — 25 paying clubs, full V1 product, founding team complete, Series A-ready.

## 3. The Customer — Who We Serve

### 3.1 Three customer segments

**Segment 1: Padel Club Operator (B2B — primary revenue)**
- **Who:** Owner-operator of 1–8 court padel facility, typically Tier 1/2 city, ₹2–8 Cr in court infra invested
- **Pain:** Player retention is fragile — once a player tries one club, switching is easy. Premium operators need differentiation. Memberships are flat; no "stickiness" tools.
- **Buying motion:** Owner makes the call; usually within 48 hours of seeing a demo
- **Price sensitivity:** Low if value proven (₹5–10k/court/month is a rounding error vs court revenue of ₹3 lakh+/month)
- **Decision criteria:** Player engagement uplift, sponsorship overlay revenue, churn reduction

**Segment 2: Padel Player (B2C — engagement + Pro upgrade)**
- **Who:** 25–55 years old, professionals, ₹15+ lakh annual income, plays 2–4 times/week, spends ₹2,000+/month on club fees
- **Pain:** Knows they're plateauing technically but can't afford ₹2,500/hr private coaching every week
- **Buying motion:** Tries free tier; converts to Pro after 2–3 months when skill-score plateau frustration kicks in
- **Price sensitivity:** Medium; ₹399/month is positioned vs. one coaching session/year
- **Decision criteria:** Specific actionable feedback, cross-club identity (Skill Score), social sharing (highlight clips)

**Segment 3: Padel Coach (B2B2C — distribution lever)**
- **Who:** Professional padel coach, 5–50 students, charges ₹1,500–3,000/hour
- **Pain:** Cannot scale beyond personal time; struggles to track student progress between sessions; loses students to YouTube and online drills
- **Buying motion:** Friend-of-friend recommendation; tries free tier; commits when they see 5+ of their students engaging with their drill recommendations
- **Price sensitivity:** Low at ₹500–1,500/month (vs. their hourly rate, this is a no-brainer)
- **Decision criteria:** Per-student progress tracking, drill assignment workflow, white-label option (their brand on the PWA)

### 3.2 The dependency triangle

```
        Club (pays)
       /            \
      /              \
  Player            Coach
 (engages)       (recommends)
       \            /
        \          /
         The Platform
```

Each side reinforces the other. A club with engaged players retains members. Players who follow coach-recommended drills improve faster. Coaches with platform-connected students get more sessions. The platform sits at the intersection — **not as a vendor, but as the connecting layer.**

## 4. The Product Offering

### 4.1 V1 product (Month 3–9)

**For clubs:**
- Single-camera install per court (₹35k hardware, recovered via subscription)
- Cloud dashboard — court usage, active player count, churn signals, monthly engagement report
- Automatic match recording — every match captured without club action
- Sponsorship overlay tools — frame-based sponsor logo placement during highlight clips

- Free tier: 3 analyses/month, basic skill score, top-3 drill recommendation
- Pro tier (₹399/month): unlimited analyses, full shot-by-shot review, skill score history, cross-club identity, share-to-Instagram highlights
- WhatsApp delivery of every match report
- Drill library — video demos for every named drill in the rubric

**For coaches:**
- Free tier: track up to 5 students
- Pro tier (₹999/month): unlimited students, custom drill assignment, white-label option, monthly per-student progress reports

- Live in-match insights (5-second delay) shown on a court-side tablet
- Tournament management module
- Cross-club leaderboards (Skill Score rankings by city)
- Match-up suggestions ("you should play this player — similar skill, complementary style")
- Sponsorship marketplace — connect sponsors with clubs by city, court traffic, player demographic

### 4.3 V3 product (Month 18–30)

- **Multi-sport expansion** — tennis (Month 18), badminton (Month 24)
- Coach-marketplace — discoverable coaches per city, with verified Skill Score uplift testimonials
- Sponsorship overlay served in real time during YouTube live streams
- Open API for tournament organisers, federations, sport publications

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### 5.1 Pricing tiers (India launch)

| Tier | Audience | Price | Inclusions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Club Standard** | 1–3 courts | ₹5,000/court/month | Hardware bundled (24-mo plan), dashboard, all-player free tier |
| **Club Premium** | 4+ courts | ₹4,000/court/month | + sponsorship tools, white-label PWA, dedicated CSM |
| **Player Free** | All players at partner clubs | ₹0 | 3 analyses/month, basic score, top-3 drill |
| **Player Pro** | Serious players | ₹399/month or ₹3,999/year | Unlimited analyses, cross-club identity, history, sharing |
| **Coach Free** | Up to 5 students | ₹0 | Track student matches |
| **Coach Pro** | Working coaches | ₹999/month | Unlimited students, drills, white-label |

### 5.2 Pricing rationale

- **Club ASP of ₹5,000/court/month** = ~₹17 in operating cost relative to court revenue (₹3 lakh+/court/month). Negligible cost-of-business; positioned as "cheaper than one missed prime-time hour of bookings"
- **Player Pro at ₹399/month** = price of one coaching session per year. Below the threshold where credit-card friction matters
- **Coach Pro at ₹999/month** = one student session per month. Easy ROI calculation

### 5.3 Pricing roadmap

| Year | Club ASP | Player Pro | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 (India) | ₹5,000/court/mo | ₹399/mo | Market establishment |
| Year 2 (India + UAE) | ₹6,000 / AED 350 | ₹499 / AED 35 | Tennis added, player base proven |
| Year 3 (multi-sport, Asia-wide) | ₹8,000 / AED 450 / SAR 350 | ₹599 / AED 45 / SAR 35 | Multi-sport, multi-country, network effects |

### 5.4 Comparison

| Service | Per-court/mo equivalent | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clutch (EU) | ~€179 / ₹17,000 | Reference incumbent; expensive in INR |
| Clutch (India price, unknown) | TBD via Sharath Kumar call | Probably ₹8,000–12,000 |

We choose to be aggressively lower-priced in India as a **deliberate moat against EU competitors** who cannot match without destroying their unit economics in their home markets.

## 7. Asia + Global Expansion Plan

### 7.1 UAE entry — Month 10–15

**Why UAE first (not KSA):**
- English-default operating language
- High court density in small geography (Dubai + Abu Dhabi covers most)
- Expat-heavy player base = high willingness-to-pay
- Easier business setup (DIFC or ADGM free zones)
- Strong AutomationXpert-style WhatsApp engagement culture

**Approach:** Acquire or partner with one existing UAE court chain (e.g., 10–20 court operator) as launch customer. Their courts become our launch installs; in exchange they get exclusive UAE distribution rights for 12 months.

**Target:** 30 UAE clubs by Month 18.

### 7.2 KSA + SE Asia — Month 18–30

**KSA approach:** Via Vision 2030 sport-infra RFPs. Bid through UAE-registered subsidiary. Partnership with a Saudi sports-tech or club-operator entity for local presence.

**SE Asia approach:** Indonesia and Thailand first. Partnership with local franchise operators (e.g., padel-court-build companies expanding into operations).

**Target by Year 3 Q4:** UAE 50 + KSA 30 + SE Asia 30 = 110 international clubs. India 400+ = total 510+ clubs.

### 7.3 Why we skip China deliberately

China padel market is large ($120M+, state-backed) but:
- Chinese competitors (Xiaoqiuquan, Lumistar, SenseTime) have ex-ByteDance / ex-DJI talent and home-field advantage
- Cultural + payment + distribution stack is fundamentally different
- Geopolitical risk (Indian companies in China face scrutiny)
- Our defensible Indian moat (procurement-policy wall vs Chinese SaaS) is built precisely on NOT being in China

**Year 3 China strategy:** License our IP + brand to a Chinese partner under revenue-share; never operate directly.

### 7.4 EU + LATAM pilots — Year 3+

Opportunistic only. EU is saturated (Clutch, Wingfield, Padelytics, Eyes-On) and not worth direct competition. LATAM (Argentina, Mexico) is open but distant.

Approach: Open API + partnership with existing operators or federations. No own-cash deployment.

## 9. Sales Playbook

### 9.1 Club sales — the 60-day pilot model

The single biggest mistake in this category is **trying to sell upfront**. We don't sell, we install. The conversion happens organically.

**Step 1 — The warm intro (Day 0):**
- Tap into Manoj's network or coach co-founder's network for the first 3 clubs in each city
- Pitch: "Free hardware install + free analytics for your entire club for 60 days. We get to validate the product, you get to see if your players engage."
- Pre-condition: club commits to introducing the platform to ≥30 of their regular players

**Step 2 — The 60-day pilot:**
- We install hardware in Week 1
- Coach co-founder visits club, runs a 1-hour "intro to your AI coach" session for members
- Every match gets analysed and feedback delivered
- Weekly check-ins with club operator on engagement metrics

**Step 3 — The conversion conversation (Day 50):**
- Show club operator their engagement dashboard: how many players are using it, churn signals reversed, etc.
- Offer: ₹5,000/court/month, hardware retention bonus (we leave the gear), 12-month commitment with 90-day exit clause
- If yes: standard SaaS contract, UPI auto-debit
- If no: we remove hardware at no cost, leave them with a player roster of "they cared, but didn't buy" insights

**Conversion target:** 70% pilot-to-paid in Year 1, rising to 85% as the case studies stack.

### 9.2 The decision-maker map (typical Indian padel club)

| Role | Influence | What they care about |
|---|---|---|
| Owner-operator (often a single person at this scale) | Decision-maker | Player retention, court utilisation, sponsor pitches |
| Head coach | Strong influence | Player development credibility, his own sessions stay relevant |
| Front-desk staff | Operational gatekeeper | "Will this make my job harder?" — must be addressed with reassurance |
| Members club committee (if it exists) | Veto power on member-facing changes | Privacy, opt-out, no surprise charges |

### 9.3 Objection handling — top 5 + responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We don't have budget for new tech" | "It's a 60-day free pilot. If your members don't engage and you don't see retention lift, you don't pay anything. Worst case for you: 60 days of free analytics." |
| "Our members value privacy" | "Members opt-in match-by-match by tapping a QR code. We don't store faces, no audio is captured. Comparable opt-in pattern as Hudle's match-tagging." |
| "We tried something similar (camera + recording) and members didn't care" | "That was passive recording. What we deliver is active coaching feedback — players see their specific weak shots and drill recommendations within 10 min of match end. Engagement is fundamentally different." |
| "Clutch / Wingfield / [other] already does this" | "Three differences: (1) we're built for Indian players in Hindi + English with WhatsApp delivery, (2) our pricing is 30–50% lower because we operate locally, (3) our coach is [name] who designed the technique rubric — not a generic algorithm." |
| "What happens when you fail / shut down?" | "Worst case for you: 60 days free, then hardware removed at no cost. Our parent founder has been running AutomationXpert for [N] years with [X] paying clients. We're capital-efficient by design, not a YC moonshot." |

## 10. Multi-Sport Expansion (Tennis, Badminton, Pickleball)

The technical architecture extends naturally (see TECHNICAL_FEASIBILITY_REPORT.md §9). The commercial logic of going multi-sport is even stronger.

### 10.1 Why expand beyond padel

| Reason | Detail |
|---|---|
| **TAM multiplier** | India padel TAM ~₹12 Cr. Adding tennis + badminton + pickleball pushes to ~₹34 Cr. |
| **Founder credibility for raise** | "Multi-sport platform" frames Series A as ₹500 Cr opportunity vs ₹50 Cr |
| **Club operator value** | Many clubs have both padel and tennis (sometimes badminton). Single-vendor multi-sport is operationally simpler |
| **Player identity reuse** | A player who plays padel + tennis maintains a single Skill Score profile across sports — high stickiness |
| **Defensive moat against single-sport competitors** | Clutch is padel-only. Adding tennis means we can beat them at clubs with mixed-sport infrastructure |

### 10.2 Sport-by-sport expansion plan

**Tennis (Month 18–24)**
- **Why second:** India has 800+ tennis academies vs ~500 padel clubs. Most acquire-curious. Public tennis CV datasets are mature (helps classifier).
- **Coach hire:** Ex-AITA or Davis Cup level
- **First clubs:** Bhupathi-network academies in Bangalore, Mumbai
- **Revenue target Year 2:** ₹5 Cr ARR from tennis alone

**Badminton (Month 24–30)**
- **Why third:** India is the world's #1 badminton market by participation (~300M+ players globally). 3,000+ Indian clubs. Massive TAM.
- **Challenge:** Shuttlecock tracking is hard (non-rigid, very different aerodynamics) — requires 6+ weeks of focused ML R&D
- **Coach hire:** Ex-PV Sindhu camp or SAI-credentialled
- **Revenue target Year 3:** ₹8 Cr ARR from badminton

**Pickleball (Year 3 onwards)**
- **Why fourth:** Fastest-growing sport in USA + emerging in UAE. Court dimensions same as badminton (reuse court keypoint detector). Small classifier delta from badminton.
- **First market:** UAE clubs (already mixed pickleball + padel facilities)
- **Revenue contribution:** Add-on to existing club subscriptions; ₹500/court/month incremental

### 10.3 Multi-sport pricing logic

A club running padel + tennis + badminton gets:
- Per-court subscription as before (₹5,000–8,000 depending on sport)
- **+15% multi-sport discount** if subscribing to 3+ sports
- **Single dashboard** for all sport courts at the club
- **Single Pro subscription for players** covering all sports they play
